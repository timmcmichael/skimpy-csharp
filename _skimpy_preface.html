Skimpy Preface

This eBook isn’t trying to to cover everything a real textbook would cover. This is a little skimpy: it covers the essentials, but just barely.

It’s intended to be a companion to my CIS162AD course at Estrella Mountain Community College. I’m an English major, and I believe reading technical information is a critical skill for programmers. But, I also recognize that many students aren’t likely to push through large chunks of assigned reading. So instead of fighting that, I’m trying to give basic explanations and examples, and then I’ll do most of the heavy lifting in videos, which are incorporated throughout this book.

Keep in mind that while some of my students are majoring in Programming and Systems Analysis or some other coding-related degree, I also have a lot of students who aren’t going to be professional coders—​it would be foolish of me to think they really need a deep dive into this stuff. And since the ones who are majoring in Programming will go on to take additional programming classes where they can build on their coding knowledge, I feel like I can get away with keeping it skimpy here.

Aside from all that, I hate making students pay for textbooks. They keep getting more and more expensive, and many of those books go into far more depth than my first-year programming students need. Those big textbooks at least offset the cost by being good reference sources—​and being that kind of comprehensive reference is not my goal.

My main goal is to help you learn the fundamentals. And my ulterior motive is to help you enjoy programming and experience the nerdy goodness of writing a cool piece of code, so that at the end of the course you are intersted in sticking with it and learning more.

And if the book sucks, at least you didn’t pay for it.
